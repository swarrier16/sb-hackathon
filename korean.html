Korean Letters: Hangul Alphabet

Unlike some other Asian languages like Chinese, with its many thousands of characters, the official alphabet for both South and North Korea - called Hangul - contains only 24 Korean letters. Because of the nature of the Korean alphabet system, these letters are super easy to read! In this guide, we'll go over each and every Korean letter of the Hangul alphabet, let you know how to pronounce each one, and talk a bit about the way the syllable block system works.

Here are the Hangul consonants, their names and the sounds they make.

End of a word: k like in talk

The vowels in Hangul, the Korean alphabet don't have names like the consonants do, instead they just have one sort of simple sound. A lot of the vowels have very similar sounds so do your best to try to figure out the difference. You'll notice that adding another line to it essentially ads a y to the sound and adding a ㅗ or a ㅜ is like adding a w sound to the vowel.
